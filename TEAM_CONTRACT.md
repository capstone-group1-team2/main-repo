## 3. Conflict Plan

### 3.1 Process for resolving disagreement
The team will address disagreements by clearly identifying the issue, allowing each teammate to explain their perspective, and discussing possible solutions respectfully. The team will aim for consensus whenever possible. If consensus cannot be reached, the team will agree on a compromise that everyone can support and document the decision.

### 3.2 If one teammate is dominating or steamrolling
- First step: Address the concern privately and respectfully, encouraging balanced participation from all team members.
- Escalation : If the issue continues, discuss it during a team meeting. If no resolution is reached, contact the Leadership Team for guidance.

### 3.3 If one teammate is under-contributing or missing check-ins
- First step: Reach out privately to understand any challenges and offer support or adjust responsibilities if needed.
- Escalation : If the issue persists without communication or improvement, discuss it with the team and, if necessary, notify the Leadership Team.

### 3.4 Handling mismatched skill levels
More experienced teammates will mentor others through pair programming, code reviews, and technical discussions without taking over their work. Less experienced teammates are encouraged to ask questions early, seek feedback, and actively participate so everyone continues learning throughout the sprint.

### 3.5 Escalation path
If the team cannot resolve a conflict internally, we will contact the Leadership Team through the course Slack channel.

### 6.2 Branch strategy

- **main:** Production-ready. All pull requests are merged into `main` after review and approval.
- **dev:** Not used. The team follows a direct feature branch → `main` workflow.
- **Feature branches:** `feature/<slug>` or `<github-handle>/<slug>`. Each branch contains one task or feature and is deleted after it is merged.

### 6.3 PR review workflow
-Every PR to main requires 2 teammate approvals before it merges. This is enforced by branch protection on main — set at Team Prepwork §4 Step 7. Not 1 approval, not 3. Two.
-PR author does not merge their own PR. Two OTHER teammates approve; either the author or a reviewer clicks Merge after the second approval lands.
-Stale-approval dismissal is on. If you push new commits to a PR after an approval, the approval resets and teammates re-review the newer code.
-PR title format: <slug>: <short imperative summary> — for example, feature/eval-harness: add held-out set loader.
-PR body includes: (a) what changed, (b) any teammate action needed, (c) test evidence (screenshot or paste of pytest -v).
-PRs auto-link to your GitHub Project board — opening a PR moves the linked card to In review; merging moves it to Done.
-No bypass exists. Branch protection is enabled with “Do not allow bypassing.” Teams of 4 always have 3 teammates other than the author available to review, so 2 approvals is achievable when the team is coordinating well. If a real emergency requires temporarily modifying the rule, open a thread in the course Slack channel naming the specific circumstance; the Lead Instructor decides whether to authorize a policy exception. Restore the rule immediately after any authorized exception. Do not modify or disable branch protection unilaterally.

### 6.4 Merge cadence
The team will merge completed work into main regularly, typically during or after the daily sync, and whenever a change is ready to unblock other teammates. When a pull request is ready for review, the author will notify the team in the Slack channel and tag the required reviewers. Merges will take place only after the required approvals have been received.

### 6.5 GitHub Project board — how the team runs it
-Columns: Backlog / In progress / In review / Done. Set at Team Prepwork §4 Step 6.
-Every task is a card. Every card has an owner (assignee). No unassigned cards in In progress.
-Automations: PR opened → In review; PR merged → Done (Set at Team Prepwork §4 Step 6.)
-Board update rhythm. When you start a task, move its card to In progress. If a card sits in In progress > 24 hours without a note, someone asks about it at standup.
Daily standup rhythm: 10:00 AM (Amman, UTC+3) on Discord. Each teammate answers: what did I finish yesterday, what will I finish today, what is blocking me. 15 minutes maximum.
